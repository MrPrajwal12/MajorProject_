Squats

Begin with proper form: stand with feet shoulder-width apart, keep back straight, engage core muscles, and maintain a neutral spine. As you squat down, push your hips back and lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as you can comfortably go. Keep your knees in line with your toes, avoid letting them collapse inward or outward. Push through your heels and engage your leg muscles to return to a standing position. Repeat for desired number of repetitions.

Push Ups

Push ups are a great exercise for building upper body strength. Begin in a plank position with your hands shoulder-width apart, fingers pointing forward. Keep your body in a straight line from head to heels. Lower your body until your chest almost touches the ground, then push up to the starting position. Keep your elbows close to your body as you lower and raise your body. Start with a few reps and gradually increase as you become stronger. Remember to engage your core and breathe deeply throughout the exercise.

Jumping-Jacks

Jumping jacks are a fun and effective exercise for improving cardiovascular fitness and coordination. Begin by standing with your feet together and arms at your sides. Jump up, spreading your legs wider than hip-width apart while simultaneously raising your arms above your head. Return to the starting position by jumping again and bringing your arms and legs back together. Repeat for several reps, keeping a steady pace. Remember to land softly on the balls of your feet and keep your core engaged throughout the exercise.

Dumbbell High Curl

Dumbbell high curls are a great exercise for building bicep strength and definition. Begin by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart and holding a dumbbell in each hand with palms facing forward. Raise the dumbbells towards your shoulders while keeping your elbows close to your body. Once the dumbbells are at shoulder level, rotate your wrists so that your palms are facing upwards. Slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position. Remember to engage your core and breathe deeply throughout the exercise.

Lunges

Lunges are a great exercise for strengthening and toning your lower body muscles. Begin by standing up straight with your feet hip-width apart. Take a big step forward with your right leg, bending your knee until your thigh is parallel to the ground. Make sure your knee doesn't go past your toes. Push off your right foot to return to the starting position, then repeat on the other leg. Keep your core engaged and your back straight throughout the exercise. Start with a few reps and gradually increase as you become stronger.
